# DM packet — Low Back Pain Mook

**Patient:** 64M, low back pain ×3 days, worse this morning. Bay 6.

**Possible truths:** mechanical (65%) · AAA with back pain (15%, **camouflaged catastrophic**) · cauda equina (10%, **camouflaged catastrophic**) · vertebral compression from metastatic disease (10%, **slow-burn occult**).

## Voice / demeanor

Stoic. "I just need something stronger than ibuprofen." Will under-report; classic male-of-a-certain-age presentation. *AAA version:* "It feels like it's tearing — but I'm sure it's just my back." (Will not volunteer the tearing description unless asked specifically about quality.) *Cauda version:* "Something feels weird about peeing — I think I just couldn't go." (Will not volunteer.) *Mets version:* mentions weight loss and night sweats only if asked.

## What this case tests

The single most useful UC tool here is **POCUS aorta** in any older patient with new back pain. It takes 15 minutes and disambiguates AAA from everything else in seconds. The neuro exam — including saddle and rectal — is what catches cauda. Mets is the slow-burn case where the cost of missing it is small in this visit but the workup launched here matters. The mechanical-low-back patient is rewarded by *not* over-imaging.

The trap: the stoic 60s male who under-reports, who looks like he just tweaked his back, and who is actually leaking. POCUS aorta is the rule that should be reflexive.
