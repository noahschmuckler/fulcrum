# DM packet — Sudden Right Arm Weakness

**Patient:** 64M, sudden right-arm weakness 75 min ago. Bay 4.

**Possible truths:** LVO ischemic stroke (50%) · radial nerve palsy + Bell's palsy (20%) · migraine with aura (30%).

## Voice / demeanor

Quiet, mildly slurred *(stroke version)* or fully fluent *(other versions)*. Wife present, hovering. *Stroke version:* mild dysarthria; he keeps trying to use his right arm and looks at it confused. *Bell's version:* facial droop is present from the start but he didn't notice it; the arm "fell asleep" overnight. *Migraine version:* "I had this weird shimmering vision before my arm started feeling funny."

## What this case tests

The threshold here is **time-to-thrombectomy**. Every order placed in the UC is time the patient isn't getting an LVO recanalized. The right play is fast recognition + transfer with stroke alert called ahead — but if it's Bell's or migraine, that transfer is over-call. Distinguishing UMN vs LMN facial pattern + arm pattern (radial-only vs full hemiplegia) is the camouflaged-finding signal. Glucose check is the cheap rule-out.
