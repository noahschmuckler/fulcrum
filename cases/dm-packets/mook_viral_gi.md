# DM packet — Viral GI Mook

**Patient:** 22M, N/V/D ×24h. Bay 4.

**Possible truths:** viral gastroenteritis (70%) · early appendicitis (25%, **camouflaged**) · new-onset DKA (5%, **deeply camouflaged**).

## Voice / demeanor

College-aged, self-conscious. "Sorry, this is gross." Wants Zofran and to go home. *DKA version:* "I've been really thirsty the last week or two." (Will not volunteer.) *Appendicitis version:* "It started kind of all over but now it's down here-ish" (gestures to RLQ).

## What this case tests

The threshold here is **bedside glucose on every "viral GI" young adult**. It costs 2 units and 2 minutes and is the entire reason DKA gets caught here vs. on the bathroom floor. McBurney + appropriate sequencing of pain migration is the appy disambiguator. The trap: the 22-year-old who "just has the stomach bug" — and the price of skipping the focused abdomen + glucose is enormous when it's wrong.
