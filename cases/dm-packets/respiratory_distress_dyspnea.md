## What this case tests

The threshold here is **PE recognition in a high-risk patient with quiet vital signs**. SpO₂ 92 with HR 108 is a soft signal in someone with a known cancer + tamoxifen. Wells, PERC, and a low-threshold POCUS lung + d-dimer are the cheap-to-order disambiguators. The trap: CHF and PE both have a tachypneic 67-year-old; the cardiac/lung exam splits them. Viral bronchitis is the camouflaged-stable case — over-imaging is the failure mode there.
